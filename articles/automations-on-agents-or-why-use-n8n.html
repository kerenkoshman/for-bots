Automations on Agents — or Why Use N8N? Behind the scenes at Darrow AI (Legal Tech Startup)— how BI & Ops uses N8N to solve communication gaps and build AI-ready workflows. A few months ago, I found myself staring at yet another operational process that was slowly draining our team’s energy. The kind that eats hours, creates human errors, and leaves you thinking: “There has to be a smarter way to do this.” That’s when I discovered N8N: an automation platform that turns repetitive workflows into automated systems. I’ve dabbled with Zapier and Make before, but N8N felt… different. More flexible, more powerful, more like building rather than configuring. To get the real picture, I sat down with Zamir Avi Dov , our Operations and Data lead at Darrow, to hear how he has been using it. Zamir and n8n BI & Ops with a Hacker’s Mindset “I’ll start with the short version: my job is to make sure Darrow wins,” Zamir said, flashing a smile. He manages company data — including performance, predictions, and data hygiene — and tackles operational headaches by breaking them down and building intelligent automations. Or, in his words: BI & Ops with a hacker’s mindset. He’s a 30-year-old lawyer who has evolved into a developer over time, living life one project at a time. And his approach to automations reflects exactly that mindset. What Makes N8N Special N8N feels like building: you can move data from point A to point B, transform it along the way, and choose between drag-and-drop or coding. Picture a ticket status updating, a new sale closing, or a backend process triggering. N8N catches the event, processes it, and sends it anywhere you want — Slack, WhatsApp, email, a database, or an API. For someone who’s spent years watching teams struggle with messy processes, this is transformative. The Real Impact For Zamir, N8N has solved problems that feel like the backbone of any growing organization. Communication gaps that used to waste time are gone. Complex calculations that previously took hours now happen automatically, eliminating repetitive manual work. He gave me examples: alerting the right people instantly when a risky event occurs in one of our legal products, or automatically passing backend teams the exact questions that arise during a sales process. Automations became the invisible glue keeping different functions aligned. How Automations Are Built Zamir’s approach is methodical. First, he observes the work, maps the processes, and identifies the pain points. Next, he sketches the ideal flow — what data moves, where it goes, and how it transforms along the way. Finally, he rolls up his sleeves and builds the workflow in N8N. “It’s part detective work, part architecture, part engineering,” he said. “And completely satisfying when it clicks.” Lessons from the Frontlines Automation isn’t “set it and forget it.” APIs change, systems update, and without monitoring, things can break silently. Zamir sets up a “watchdog automation” that notifies a Slack channel whenever an issue occurs, including the cause and a direct link to the error. He also simplifies where others overcomplicate. Duplicates? N8N has a built-in deduplication node. Sending multiple API requests? Process them in batches to avoid hitting rate limits. The result is automations that are robust, reliable, and actually reduce friction rather than create new headaches. Where AI Fits In Of course, the next question was obvious: what about AI? Zamir’s advice is practical. Start with a use case that clearly delivers value. Use N8N as the orchestration layer, and integrate AI agents only where the margin for error is low. Summarizing text, classifying items, or explaining code snippets are all good fits. Why It Matters If you lead teams, build products, or want operations to run smoothly, automation isn’t a “nice-to-have.” It’s foundational. For me, N8N is one of those tools that shifts perspective. Suddenly, what used to feel like endless busywork becomes a canvas for smarter processes. And that, more than anything, is why I keep coming back to it.
