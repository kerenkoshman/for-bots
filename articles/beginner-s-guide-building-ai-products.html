Founders and product managers come to me with the same question: How do you even start building an AI product? After having this conversation more than a few times and combining it with my personal passion for mental models and best practices, I wrote down the complete guide for the beginner AI product manager. Let’s start with the bottom line, the six essential steps: Define the business problem — Every product starts with a real user problem that impacts the business. Without this, even the most innovative model won’t help. Define product metrics — What exactly are we trying to move? What will tell us we’re actually making an impact? Define model evaluation — How do we know if the model is working? (I wrote about this in detail here , worth a read!) Data collection, cleaning, and labeling — This is where domain experts come in. Data quality = model quality. Release an MVP — Ship an initial version, even if it’s not perfect, to start learning quickly. Iterate — The world is vast, users surprise us, and the learning never stops. This is where the real magic happens. While steps 1,2,5,6 are what every good PM already knows, steps 3 and 4 are unique to AI. These require dedicated understanding. I believe the best way to learn is hands-on experimentation and by learning from others’ experiences. That’s why these are the courses I’d recommend if you want to really get it: The basics: Understand what generative AI models are → Generative AI for Everyone A slightly deeper dive: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7xTGNNLPyMI Go straight to the spiritual father, Andrew Ng → Machine Learning Specialization If you want the technical side, Hugging Face has excellent courses. To wrap up: Don’t let the dizzying feeling make you bury your head in the sand 🙂 The time to get hands-on is right now . Learn the limitations, learn the capabilities, and figure out how you can personally use AI, and how you can start building these kinds of products.
